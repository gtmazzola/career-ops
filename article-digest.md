## Battery Swapping Standards -- Regional Policy and Standards Analysis

**Hero metrics:** 3-country comparative scope, policy and standards analysis across Indonesia, Thailand, and Malaysia

**Scope:** Comparative analytical note on battery swapping policy, interoperability, standardisation gaps, and second-life battery opportunities in Southeast Asia

**Key decisions:**
- Treated battery swapping as an entry point for regional standards alignment rather than a narrow infrastructure question
- Focused on interoperability, regulatory divergence, and investment confidence
- Linked circular economy considerations to second-life battery deployment and wider energy system value

**Proof points:**
- Synthesised policy and standards developments across three ASEAN countries
- Assessed IEC-related standardisation gaps and implications for regional harmonisation
- Used as policy-facing analytical support for UNIDO and regional consultation work

## Hydrogen and Power-to-X -- GTCC Article

**Hero metrics:** Published external article, co-authored with UNIDO Representative-level involvement

**Scope:** Policy-facing article for the German-Thai Chamber of Commerce on hydrogen, Power-to-X, industrial decarbonization, and application prioritisation

**Key decisions:**
- Used the Hydrogen Ladder to distinguish high-value uses from less strategic applications

**Proof points:**

- Connected hydrogen deployment choices to inclusive and sustainable industrial development
- Publicly acknowledged by UNIDO Sub-Regional Office in Thailand

## Hybrid SMR-Solar PV-Storage System Optimisation

**Hero metrics:** 500 MW off-grid AI data centre case, 5-SMR versus 6-SMR scenario comparison, 15-minute resolution over 96 time steps

**Architecture:** Constrained cost-minimisation model in GAMSPy combining NuScale SMRs, solar PV, LFP battery storage, and thermal storage across electricity, hydrogen, storage, and cooling cost components

**Key decisions:**
- Compared smaller and larger SMR configurations across on-grid and off-grid cases rather than assuming a single optimal design
- Integrated dispatch, storage scheduling, and cost comparison in a single optimisation framework
- Tested sensitivity to grid access assumptions, load variation, and major cost parameters

**Proof points:**
- Found that additional nuclear capacity reduced grid dependence but increased total system cost
- Showed that the smaller configuration was more cost-effective where reliable grid access existed
- Combined optimisation modelling with nuclear thermal-hydraulic simulation and solar generation modelling

## Solar PV Carport Techno-Economic Analysis

**Hero metrics:** 2,470 kW system, 4,050 monocrystalline panels, approximately EUR 2.2M total investment

**Scope:** Techno-economic assessment of a solar PV carport for Interporto Bologna using PVsyst, system configuration analysis, and financial comparison across pricing structures

**Key decisions:**
- Modelled irradiance and system design together rather than treating technical performance and economics separately
- Compared market-price and PPA revenue scenarios over the project lifetime
- Focused sensitivity analysis on discount rate and panel degradation as the main cost drivers

**Proof points:**
- Calculated LCOE and full CAPEX/OPEX breakdown
- Identified the PPA scenario as more favourable over the life of the project
- Produced decision-useful analysis linking system design choices to commercial viability
