# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

Reusable interview stories mapped to common competency questions. Each story can be adapted to multiple roles by adjusting the framing. Maintained by career-ops — new stories added from Block F evaluations.

## How to use

1. Before an interview, review this file — pick 3-4 stories that map to the role
2. Each story has **Adaptations** showing how to frame it for different role types
3. The "Big Three" questions can all be answered from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine Stories 1, 5, and 3 into a narrative arc
   - "Most impactful project" → Story 1 (EUR 12M proposal) or Story 2 (APFSD13)
   - "Conflict/challenge you resolved" → Story 2 (5 agencies) or Story 6 (3 countries)

## 1. EUR 12M IKI Climate & Biodiversity Proposal

**Source:** Reports #001, #027, #030, #046 — used across Deloitte, RMI, UNOPS, ERM evaluations
**Best for:** Project management, stakeholder coordination, structured analytical work, proposal development, working under pressure, cross-country coordination

**S:** UNIDO needed to develop a major climate and biodiversity funding proposal for the IKI Large Grants 2025 window, spanning three countries (Malaysia, Philippines, Brazil) with multiple government and institutional partners.

**T:** Draft and structure the full concept note, evidence base, budget, and stakeholder engagement strategy, and coordinate government endorsement letters and the complete submission package — all under a tight donor deadline.

**A:** Led the proposal development end-to-end: structured the concept note around biodiversity-integrated investment appraisal using COMFAR methodology, coordinated inputs from government counterparts across three countries, drafted the budget and results framework, and managed the endorsement letter process.

**R:** Delivered a complete EUR 12M proposal package for IKI submission, with all required government endorsements and institutional sign-offs secured on time.

**Reflection:** Cross-country coordination requires early alignment on scope — the most productive approach was sending a clear written brief for partners to react to rather than starting with open-ended consultations. Template-first saves rework.

**Adaptations:**
- *Consulting:* "Multi-stakeholder advisory engagement with 3 country-level clients"
- *IO/UN:* "Donor proposal development with inter-agency coordination"
- *Corporate:* "Structured project delivery under institutional constraints"

## 2. SDG 9 Round Table — APFSD13 (5 UN Agencies)

**Source:** Reports #001, #007, #027, #030 — used across Deloitte, ESCAP, RMI, UNOPS evaluations
**Best for:** Multi-stakeholder coordination, communication, leadership, event management, analytical synthesis, working with senior stakeholders

**S:** The 13th Asia-Pacific Forum on Sustainable Development required a structured SDG 9 Round Table session with substantive contributions from five UN agencies (UNIDO, ESCAP, ITU, ILO, UNOPS).

**T:** Develop the concept note and research agenda, coordinate inter-agency inputs, manage all logistics (speaker coordination, Note Verbales, moderator briefing, breakout groups), and produce all written outputs.

**A:** Designed the session structure with three thematic breakout groups, coordinated substantive contributions from all five agencies, aggregated and analysed ESCAP SDG 9 statistical data, prepared government panelist invitations and moderator briefing packages, and managed the full event logistics.

**R:** Successfully delivered the Round Table. Authored the Summary Report (ESCAP Addendum input), Rapporteur's Statement for the APFSD13 plenary, and policy recommendation synthesis across six thematic areas. Delivered UNIDO inputs to the inter-agency lessons-learned debrief.

**Reflection:** Multi-agency coordination works best with a clear written brief that agencies can react to — open-ended requests produce delays. The structured breakout format generated more actionable recommendations than a single plenary discussion would have.

**Adaptations:**
- *Consulting:* "Managed a multi-client workshop producing actionable deliverables"
- *IO/UN:* "Led inter-agency coordination for a regional policy forum"
- *Research:* "Synthesized statistical data and policy inputs into structured analytical outputs"

## 3. Thailand EV & Battery Regulatory Database

**Source:** Reports #001, #012, #046 — used across Deloitte, AIIB, ERM evaluations
**Best for:** Analytical skills, regulatory analysis, initiative, creating reusable tools, policy research, attention to detail

**S:** UNIDO's energy transition programming in Thailand lacked a structured view of the EV and battery policy landscape — regulations were scattered across multiple government agencies and jurisdictions.

**T:** Build a comprehensive regulatory database mapping Thailand's EV and battery policy framework to support policy research on e-mobility and energy transition, including for a UNCT Outcome 1 session.

**A:** Conducted comparative analysis across multiple regulatory domains (incentives, standards, safety, import/export, grid integration), structured findings into a searchable database format, and mapped which agencies own which regulations.

**R:** Delivered a regulatory database now used across UNIDO's energy transition programming in Thailand. The structured format makes ongoing updates efficient as new policies are enacted.

**Reflection:** Regulatory mapping reveals more gaps than expected — the value isn't just documenting what exists but making visible what's missing. A structured database approach turns a one-time research exercise into a reusable institutional tool.

**Adaptations:**
- *Consulting:* "Built a regulatory landscape assessment for a client in an evolving policy environment"
- *ESG/Corporate:* "Conducted regulatory due diligence for energy transition compliance"
- *Research:* "Developed a structured policy database for comparative regulatory analysis"

## 4. PCP Documentation — Theory of Change & LogFrame (Thailand & Lao PDR)

**Source:** Reports #021, #022, #030 — used across UNIDO, UNOPS evaluations
**Best for:** Results-based management, programme design, government engagement, analytical documentation, working across countries

**S:** Two Programme for Country Partnership programmes (Thailand and Lao PDR) needed comprehensive results-based documentation to guide implementation and secure stakeholder buy-in from four government counterparts.

**T:** Develop the full programme documentation suite — concept notes, workplans, Theory of Change, LogFrame, and IRPF indicators — synthesizing inputs from government (MOI, NESDC, EECO, IEAT) and private-sector stakeholders.

**A:** Built the ToC linking programme activities to development outcomes, structured the LogFrame with measurable indicators at output/outcome level, drafted workplans, and facilitated input from government counterparts through structured consultation.

**R:** Delivered complete PCP documentation for both country programmes, with indicator frameworks accepted by government partners.

**Adaptations:**

## 5. Hydrogen & Power-to-X Article — German-Thai Chamber of Commerce

**Source:** Reports #001, #012, #027 — used across Deloitte, AIIB, RMI evaluations
**Best for:** Communication, thought leadership, translating complexity, working with senior leaders, external visibility, energy expertise

**S:** UNIDO Bangkok needed to communicate its position on hydrogen and Power-to-X to the business and policy community in Thailand, but the topic is technically complex and politically sensitive (blue vs green hydrogen debate).

**T:** Co-author an accessible article for the German-Thai Chamber of Commerce publication that translates the complex hydrogen policy landscape into clear guidance for investors, policymakers, and business readers.

**A:** Used the Hydrogen Ladder framework to distinguish high-value from low-value hydrogen applications, framed blue hydrogen as a transitional step while maintaining focus on green hydrogen scale-up, connected hydrogen deployment choices to inclusive and sustainable industrial development, and co-authored with UNIDO Representative-level involvement.

**R:** Published article in the GTCC publication. Publicly acknowledged by UNIDO Sub-Regional Office in Thailand on social media.

**Reflection:** Policy communication is most effective when it connects technical choices to business decisions. The Hydrogen Ladder provided a simple framework that made a complex topic accessible without oversimplifying.

**Adaptations:**
- *Consulting:* "Delivered thought leadership content for an institutional client's external engagement"
- *Research:* "Translated technical energy analysis into policy-facing publication"
- *Corporate:* "Created stakeholder-facing content on emerging energy technology"

**T:** Prepare an analytical note assessing regulatory divergence, IEC standardisation gaps, and second-life battery deployment opportunities across the three countries.

**A:** Conducted comparative analysis of battery swapping policy and standards across all three countries, assessed IEC-related standardisation gaps and implications for regional harmonisation, and linked circular economy considerations (second-life batteries for grid-scale storage) to the investment case.

**R:** Delivered an analytical note used as policy-facing support for UNIDO and regional consultation work on e-mobility standards in the IMT-GT subregion.

**Reflection:** Treating battery swapping as an entry point for regional standards alignment rather than a narrow infrastructure question opened up the analysis to circular economy and investment confidence dimensions that proved more actionable.

**Adaptations:**
- *Consulting:* "Three-country regulatory benchmarking study for regional market entry"

## 7. Hybrid SMR-Solar PV-Storage Optimisation

**Source:** Reports #012, #027 — used across AIIB, RMI evaluations
**Best for:** Quantitative skills, technical depth, energy systems, modelling, problem-solving, research capability

**S:** As part of an M.Sc. research project at Politecnico di Milano, needed to determine the cost-optimal configuration for a hybrid nuclear-renewable energy system serving a 500 MW off-grid AI data centre.

**T:** Build a constrained cost-minimisation model integrating multiple energy sources (NuScale SMRs, solar PV, battery storage, thermal storage) and evaluate different configurations across operational scenarios.

**A:** Developed the optimization model in GAMSPy across LCOE, LCOH, LCOS, cooling, and carbon cost components. Optimised dispatch and storage scheduling at 15-minute resolution over 96 time steps. Compared 5-SMR and 6-SMR configurations across on-grid and off-grid scenarios. Conducted nuclear thermal-hydraulic simulation (Modelica/Dymola) and solar generation modelling (PVsyst). Performed sensitivity analysis on load variation and cost parameters.

**R:** Found that the 6-SMR configuration reduced grid dependence but increased LCOE; the 5-SMR design was more cost-effective when grid access was available — highlighting the sensitivity of optimal sizing to grid reliability assumptions.

**Reflection:** Adding capacity has diminishing returns once a reliability threshold is met. The model's value was in quantifying exactly where that threshold sits under different assumptions — making it a decision tool rather than just an academic exercise.

**Adaptations:**
- *Energy/MDB:* "Built a techno-economic model to evaluate clean energy investment options"
- *Consulting:* "Developed a quantitative decision framework for energy infrastructure configuration"
- *Research:* "Conducted original optimization research on hybrid energy systems"
